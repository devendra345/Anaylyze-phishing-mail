# Identified Phishing Indicators (Red Flags)

### 1. Sender Identity Mismatch (Email Spoofing)

The email claims to be from the **Vice Chancellor, Alan Shepard**, as indicated in the signature. However, the sender's email address is **[223110007@student.uj.ac.za](mailto:223110007@student.uj.ac.za)**, which belongs to a student account. Official university communications from senior administrators are not typically sent from student email addresses. This discrepancy strongly suggests sender impersonation or spoofing.

### 2. Email Client Security Warning

The email system displays a warning banner stating:

*"Some people who received this message don't often get email from [223110007@student.uj.ac.za](mailto:223110007@student.uj.ac.za)."*

Such warnings indicate that the sender is unusual or unrecognized, which can be a sign of a potentially malicious or phishing email.

### 3. Generic Greeting

The message begins with the generic salutation **"Good Day"** instead of addressing the recipient by name. Legitimate university communications are often personalized, whereas phishing emails commonly use generic greetings to target a large number of recipients simultaneously.

### 4. Social Engineering Through Financial Incentives

The email attempts to exploit students' financial concerns by offering a flexible part-time job requiring only **5–15 hours per week**. The promise of easy income to help cover tuition expenses is a common social engineering tactic used to attract victims and lower their suspicion.

### 5. Suspicious Call to Action

The recipient is encouraged to click an **"Apply Here"** hyperlink to apply for the opportunity. Phishing emails frequently use embedded links that redirect users to fraudulent websites designed to steal login credentials, personal information, or financial data.

## Conclusion

Based on the sender mismatch, security warning, generic greeting, financial lure, and suspicious hyperlink, this email exhibits multiple characteristics commonly associated with phishing attacks. Recipients should avoid clicking any links, downloading attachments, or providing personal information and should report the email to the university's IT security team.
